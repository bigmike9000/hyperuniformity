\begin{center}
  {\LARGE Ranking Hyperuniform One-Dimensional Point Patterns}\\[2em]
  Michael Fang\\
  Advisor: Professor Salvatore Torquato\\[1em]
  Spring 2026
\end{center}

\vspace{2em}

Hyperuniform point patterns, characterized by anomalously suppressed large-scale density
fluctuations, arise across a diverse range of physical contexts from quasicrystals to disordered
packings. Despite substantial theoretical progress, the existing catalog of characterized
one-dimensional hyperuniform structures remains sparse, particularly for Class I systems
($\alpha > 1$). This project aims to provide a much more comprehensive ranking of 1D
hyperuniform point configurations by systematically computing the hyperuniformity exponent
$\alpha$ and surface-area coefficient $\bar\Lambda$ across quasicrystals and exotic disordered
systems.

A key motivation for this project is that hyperuniform systems often exhibit unusual physical
properties, including anomalous transport, photonic band gaps, and mechanical rigidity that
correlate with their structural metrics. Establishing a comprehensive $(\alpha, \bar\Lambda)$
ranking also lays groundwork for making these connections more precise.

The paper will start by introducing hyperuniformity and its two complementary
characterizations, being the real-space number variance $\sigma^2(R)$ and the structure factor
$S(k) \sim |k|^\alpha$. Next, we will go over the classification into Classes I--III via the
exponent $\alpha$ and the role of the surface-area coefficient $\bar\Lambda$ as a measure of
order within each class. We will then describe the computational methodology, including pattern
generation by the substitution and projection methods for quasicrystals, as well as algorithms
for producing disordered hyperuniform configurations such as stealthy hyperuniform patterns and
perturbed lattices. We will also discuss how the number variance and exponent $\alpha$ are
computed through the sliding-window method and diffusion spreadability.

Results on the five metallic-mean quasicrystals (Fibonacci, silver, bronze, copper, and nickel
chains) have confirmed Class I hyperuniformity with the theoretically predicted universal
exponent $\alpha = 3$, as well as given the ordering
$\bar\Lambda_{\rm Fib} < \bar\Lambda_{\rm Ag} < \bar\Lambda_{\rm Br} < \bar\Lambda_{\rm Cu} < \bar\Lambda_{\rm Ni}$.
These results are complemented by an expanded catalog of disordered hyperuniform systems,
including stealthy hyperuniform point patterns and perturbed lattices, compiled into a unified
$(\alpha, \bar\Lambda)$ ranking table. The paper will conclude by discussing the implications
of our rankings for hyperuniform 1D point patterns, and the extent to which $\alpha$ and
$\bar\Lambda$ distinguish structurally distinct systems.
